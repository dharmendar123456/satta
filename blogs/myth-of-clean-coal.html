The Myth of Clean Coal

By An Anonymous 10th Grader | Published on June 28, 2026

If you listen to certain politicians during an election year, you will undoubtedly hear them talk about saving the mining industry through the miracle of "clean coal." They paint a picture of a technological breakthrough that allows us to keep digging up the dirtiest fossil fuel on Earth without any of the environmental consequences. But as a teenager who actually reads the scientific data instead of just listening to political campaign speeches, I can tell you the harsh reality: "clean coal" is an absolute myth. It is a marketing term invented by the coal industry to avoid being shut down by environmental regulations.

What is "Clean Coal" Supposed to Be?

The term "clean coal" usually refers to a technology called Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS). The idea is that when a power plant burns coal to generate electricity, massive scrubbers in the smokestacks capture the carbon dioxide before it escapes into the atmosphere. The CO2 is then compressed into a liquid and pumped deep underground into geological formations. In theory, it sounds great. But in practice, the technology is astronomically expensive and highly inefficient. Despite decades of research and billions of dollars in government subsidies, there are almost zero commercial coal plants in the world that successfully capture 100% of their emissions. The process itself requires a massive amount of energy, which means the plant has to burn even more coal just to run the carbon capture machines.

The Dirty Reality of Mining

Even if carbon capture technology worked perfectly and was cheap to implement, it completely ignores the other devastating impacts of coal. You cannot "cleanly" blow the top off a mountain with explosives to extract the coal inside. Mountaintop removal mining destroys entire ecosystems, fills in valleys, and severely pollutes the local water supply with heavy metals like arsenic and lead. When coal is washed and prepared for burning, it creates millions of gallons of toxic coal ash sludge, which is often stored in massive, unlined pits that frequently leak into rivers. None of these horrific environmental disasters are solved by putting a filter on a smokestack.

A Political Distraction

The entire concept of clean coal is a political distraction. It gives politicians an excuse to continue supporting a dying, 19th-century industry instead of making the hard decision to transition coal workers into the modern, renewable energy sector. We don't need to waste billions of dollars trying to make the dirtiest fuel source slightly less dirty. We already have solar, wind, and geothermal energy. It is time for our political leaders to stop selling us the myth of clean coal and start investing in the genuinely clean energy of the future.
